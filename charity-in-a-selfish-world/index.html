/pa-nà-ro/

noun

Braided wicker basket of hemispherical or cylindrical shape with a curved handle. The term derives from the Latin panarum which indicated a basket for storing bread. Although in the countryside it still retains its primitive function, in the city it performs much more complex and creative transport tasks. Using it is very simple, just tie a strong rope to its handle and, if necessary, lower it like an elevator, holding the rest of the rope securely with your hands and paying attention to the heads of passers-by and not to get it caught in the wires of the clothes hanging of the tenants on the lower floors.

Italian public healthcare’s budget has been cut

In the decade 2010-2019, various governments have cut the investments and spending increases expected for the healthcare system.

€ 37 billion are the healthcare spending increases planned for these years that have been cut.

Minister Balduzzi declares that in the 2012-2015 period the sum of various financial manoeuvres relating to the period 2010-2012 will subtract from the NHS a figure close to € 25 billion, the correct figure being raised by the Regions which estimate the cuts in approximately € 30 billion.

December 17, 2012

The 2014 Stability Law reduces healthcare funding by over € 1 billion: € 540 million in 2015 and € 610 million in 2016.

December 20, 2013

The 2015 Stability Law asks the Regions for a contribution to public finance of € 4 billion.

October 16, 2014

TThe State-Regions Conference reaches an agreement on the proposed agreement for cuts to health: € 2,352 million for 2015 and 2016.

July 2, 2015

With the Local Authorities Decree the funding of the NHS for the years 2015 and 2016 is reduced overall by € 6.79 billion compared to the provisions of the "Patto per la Saluta".
