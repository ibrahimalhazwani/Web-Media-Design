Braided wicker basket of hemispherical or cylindrical shape with a curved handle. The term derives from the Latin panarum which indicated a basket for storing bread. Although in the countryside it still retains its primitive function, in the city it performs much more complex and creative transport tasks. Using it is very simple, just tie a strong rope to its handle and, if necessary, lower it like an elevator, holding the rest of the rope securely with your hands and paying attention to the heads of passers-by and not to get it caught in the wires of the clothes hanging of the tenants on the lower floors.

Italian public healthcare’s budget has been cut

In the decade 2010-2019, various governments have cut the investments and spending increases expected for the healthcare system.

€ 37 billion are the healthcare spending increases planned for these years that have been cut.

Minister Balduzzi declares that in the 2012-2015 period the sum of various financial manoeuvres relating to the period 2010-2012 will subtract from the NHS a figure close to € 25 billion, the correct figure being raised by the Regions which estimate the cuts in approximately € 30 billion.

December 17, 2012

The 2014 Stability Law reduces healthcare funding by over € 1 billion: € 540 million in 2015 and € 610 million in 2016.

December 20, 2013

The 2015 Stability Law asks the Regions for a contribution to public finance of € 4 billion.

October 16, 2014

The State-Regions Conference reaches an agreement on the proposed agreement for cuts to health: € 2,352 million for 2015 and 2016.

July 2, 2015

With the Local Authorities Decree the funding of the NHS for the years 2015 and 2016 is reduced overall by € 6.79 billion compared to the provisions of the "Patto per la Saluta".

August 4, 2015

The 2017 Budget Law redistributes the resources already allocated by the agreement 11 February 2016 to the 2017-2018 two-year period: € 113 billion for 2017, € 114 billion for 2018 and € 115 billion for 2019.

December 21, 2016

The DEF 2017 forecasts that the health expenditure / GDP ratio will decrease from 6.7% in 2017 to 6.4% in 2019.

April 11, 2017

The DM "Rideterminazione del livello del fabbisogno sanitario nazionale" reduces the public funding of the NHS by € 423 million for the year 2017 and by € 604 million for the year 2018 and subsequent.

June 5, 2017

NADEF 2017 estimates a further reduction in the health expenditure / GDP ratio from 6.6% in 2017 to 6.3% in 2020.

September 23, 2017

The 2018 Budget Law does not provide for any increase in the FSN which remains at € 114,396 million.

December 27, 2017

The DEF 2018 confirms the progressive reduction in the health expenditure / GDP ratio, extending to 6.36 the 20% already estimated for 2020 in NADEF 2017.

April 26, 2018

In the face of more than optimistic economic growth forecasts, NADEF 2018 increases the health expenditure / GDP ratio by only 0.1% per year (6.5% in 2019 and 6.4% in 2020 and 2021), effectively denying the expected turnaround announced by Premier Conte on the occasion of the confidence speech.

September 27, 2018

The Budget Law 2019 confirms € 1 billion increase in the FSN and provides for an increase of € 2 billion for 2020 and a further € 1.5 billion for 2021, subject to the stipulation, by March 31, 2019, of a State-Regions Agreement for the 2019-2021 Health Pact that provides for "planning and improvement measures for the quality of care and services provided and for cost efficiency".

The situation in Italy

The ongoing COVID-19 pandemic was first confirmed to have spread to Italy on 31 January 2020, when two Chinese tourists in Rome tested positive for the virus.

As of 6 May 2020, Italy has 91,528 active cases, one of the highest in the world. Overall there have been 214,457 confirmed cases and 29,684 deaths, while there have been 85,231 recoveries or dismissals.
